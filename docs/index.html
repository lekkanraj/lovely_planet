Few Words About Us

In the early days of humanity, we observed nature in its various facets. This included the movement of heavenly bodies, the flight patterns of birds, changes in weather patterns, ocean waves and flow of water in water bodies, shedding of leaves in trees and autumnal change of color in leaves, the life cycles of plants and animals and their behaviors. Their life was tied closely to nature.

Mother Earth

Many discoveries and inventions are from human observations of nature. However, in recent times, instead of using nature as a teacher, we have started exploiting it for our own development. Humankind is now on the path of actively destroying our Mother Earth.

Our Mission

It is our mission to halt this process of destruction. We are a team of energetic and young people, who aim to teach the new generation to observe nature, promote learning of basic sciences, respect and worship nature and protect living beings and the Earth from destruction to make a better tomorrow.

ASTRONOMY

Astronomy, the study of planets and stars is the oldest science. From the earliest days of human civilization, people used to observe the sky, relate it with their culture, and do calculations to predict the future positions of the heavenly bodies. Our team will promote astronomy through class room sessions, one- day workshops, night sky observations and many other activities.

BIRD WATCHING

Bird watching or birding is a recreational activity open to all age group of people. The migrations and habits of birds tell us a great deal about the changing conditions in the environment and the availability of essentials such as food and water. Of course, birds are also a beautiful and diverse group of animals which are easier to view than other kinds of creatures. Bird watching can be done with the naked eye, using binoculars and telescopes or by listening for bird sounds.

OBSERVE NATURE

Human beings have to depend on nature for everything. What could be more wonderful than observing the cycle of the blooming of flowers, the bees seeking out flowers for nectar and pollinating them, leading to the next cycle? There are many such cycles, such as the flow of rivers into the sea, and the river again becoming robust due to monsoon downpours. These, and other cycles such as the changes in plant group and migratory patterns of fishes and birds let us know the state of the environment.

PLANTING TREES

Climate change is affecting every part of this world and temperatures have been soaring up in many regions across the world. The release of carbon dioxide- 〖CO〗_2 from factories, vehicles and industries is a major contributing factor to global warming. Carbon dioxide prevents the Sun’s rays which have entered the Earth’s atmosphere from escaping by forming a blanket in the atmosphere. Trees are the natural weapon to control this effect, as they remove carbon dioxide from the atmosphere and give out oxygen. Planting more trees helps in fighting global warming and climate change.

OLYMPIAD EXAM TRAINING

Olympiads are competitive exams conducted at the school level for students, based on the school curriculum. The present world is more competitive; these exams help in assessing the potential of children and prepare themselves for challenges in the future.

APTITUDE TRAINING

Aptitude tests are a way of testing a candidate for his/her abilities to do certain tasks and react to different situations. The most common types of tests are numerical reasoning, logical reasoning, quantitative aptitude and data interpretation. We train candidates for all these variants of aptitude tests and guide people towards career development.
